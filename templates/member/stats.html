Educational Content

BMI Explained: Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It's a general indicator of weight-related health risks. However, it doesn't account for muscle mass or body composition. Learn more about BMI.

BMR Explained: Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the estimated number of calories your body burns at rest to maintain basic functions like breathing, circulation, and organ function. It's influenced by factors like age, sex, height, weight, and body composition (muscle mass burns more calories than fat). Knowing your BMR provides a starting point for understanding your daily calorie needs. Learn more about BMR.

TDEE Explained: Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the total number of calories your body burns in a day, including your BMR and the calories burned through activity. This includes exercise, daily movement (like walking or housework), and even the thermic effect of food (calories burned digesting and processing food). Your TDEE is a more accurate reflection of your overall calorie needs compared to just your BMR. To lose weight, you need to consume fewer calories than your TDEE; to gain weight, you need to consume more. Learn more about TDEE.
